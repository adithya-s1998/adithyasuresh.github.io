Overview

The Crime Scene as Classroom

Forensic training traditionally relies on physical mock crime scenes — expensive to set up, impossible to reset, and limited in scenario variety. This VR solution allows trainees to enter fully reconstructed crime scenes, make procedural decisions with real consequences, and repeat scenarios to build proficiency.

Every object in the scene is interactive. Evidence can be collected, bagged, photographed, and logged — mirroring real-world CSI procedure with a guided but non-prescriptive workflow.

Interaction Design

Procedural VR Interactions

Core interactions were designed around realism and pedagogical intent. Trainees must physically crouch to examine floor-level evidence, use gesture-based tools (UV light, fingerprint kit, evidence bags), and follow correct tagging procedures before moving items.

Experience

Investigating the Scene

The full crime scene walkthrough — from initial perimeter assessment to final evidence log — was designed to take approximately 20 minutes, with branching outcomes depending on what evidence the trainee finds and how correctly they process it.
