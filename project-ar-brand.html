01 — Brief

An in-store Experience

An emerging jewelry brand specialising in lab-grown diamonds was opening their first physical retail store and wanted to position themselves as an innovative, forward-thinking brand. The brief was to design and prototype an in-store immersive experience that could educate, engage and delight customers — bridging the gap between the novelty of their product and the trust required for a high-involvement purchase.

Context
Lab Grown Diamond Brand

Opening their first retail shop, positioning as an innovative, ethical brand.

Challenge
Build Trust Through Experience

Lab-grown diamonds face skepticism — the in-store experience needed to educate and build confidence.

Goal
Immersive In-Store Solutions

Design and prototype AR-powered interactions that feel premium and are genuinely useful.

Brand Experience & Immersive Tech

Immersive Tech in Retail

Companies advertise their AR/VR practices as an innovative feature — utilising the technologies to establish their brands and send the message that they stand for innovation, exploration, and creativity. Research by Boletsis and Karahasanovic (2018) shows that AR has a much wider penetration in retail compared to VR.

Potential Barriers

Cost & Maintenance
High cost of implementation, ongoing maintenance, updates and repairs create adoption friction for smaller brands.
Privacy Concerns
Use of cameras and sensors raises user privacy concerns, requiring transparent data practices.
Staff Acceptance
Resistance or unfamiliarity from retail staff can undermine the experience before it reaches the customer.
System Compatibility
Integrating AR solutions with existing POS and retail management systems can be technically complex.

Understanding the Product — Lab Grown Diamonds

Process
Made using either Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) or High Pressure High Temperature (HPHT) process — replicating the conditions that form natural diamonds.
Ecological
Considered ecologically friendly since there is no mining process involved — significantly lower carbon footprint than mined diamonds.
Ethical
Don't carry the stigma of conflict diamonds — making them a more ethical choice for socially conscious consumers.
Adoption
Not yet widely accepted compared to natural diamonds — consumer education remains a critical part of the brand's role.

Key Takeaways

  • Brands are embracing immersive technology as a competitive differentiator.
  • Customers spend more time in retail stores when provided with immersive experiences.
  • People are more likely to engage with brands that provide interactive experiences over those that do not.
  • There is considerable scepticism and uncertainty about lab-grown diamonds' authenticity, quality and environmental impact.
  • Ethical considerations and cost are major factors influencing potential purchase decisions.
05 — Concepts

Two Interaction Concepts

Two distinct in-store interaction concepts emerged from the research — each addressing a different user need identified in the surveys.

Concept 01
Ring Configurator with AR Try-On

An in-store system where customers can personalise their jewellery — customising the ring base, diamond shape and metal — then virtually try it on using an AR marker before placing an order.

Concept 02
Phygital Interactive In-Store Display

An interactive information kiosk where customers can get educated about the brand, its history and the product — addressing the knowledge gap around lab-grown diamonds through engaging, touch-driven content.

06 — Design & Development

Ring Configurator with AR Try-On

The Ring Configurator was developed across three interconnected scenes — each designed to guide the customer from personalisation through to understanding, with the AR try-on moment as the centrepiece.

01
Ring Customisation Scene

User can customise the ring base, diamond shape and ring metal. Real-time 3D preview updates as selections are made.

02
AR Try-On Scene

The user wears a target marker on their finger and matches it to the on-screen reference — triggering the customised 3D ring to appear augmented on top of the marker.

03
Know Your Diamond

An educational scene that helps users understand how similar lab-grown diamonds are to natural ones — presented through the 4C's of diamond quality.

User Journey

The physical setup consists of a tablet running the application, with a fixed overhead camera.

Technology

Game Engine
Unity 3D

Unity was chosen for its straightforward implementation of AR features and user-friendly build process for Android. Despite challenges achieving high realism in diamond render quality, Unity's versatility and robust AR support made it the best overall choice.

AR Tracking
Vuforia Model Tracking

Vuforia's model tracking uses a 3D-printed object as the target marker — giving very stable tracking even during hand movement. The augmented ring moves with the marker naturally. The key constraint: the camera needs to be close and the marker orientation must precisely match the predefined position.
