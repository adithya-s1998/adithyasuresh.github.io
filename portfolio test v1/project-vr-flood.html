Overview

Learning by Doing — in VR

Traditional flood safety training relies on pamphlets and drills that fail to create the kind of visceral, muscle-memory responses needed in real emergencies. This project explores how VR can place trainees directly inside a flood scenario — forcing them to make real-time decisions under realistic environmental pressure.

The simulation covers three critical scenarios: domestic flooding, street-level flash floods, and vehicle submersion — each with branching decision trees that adapt based on user actions.

UX Design

Designing for Presence

VR UX presents unique constraints — no screen edges, no traditional UI, and a user who must feel genuinely immersed. The interface was built entirely in diegetic space: prompts appear as physical signs, timers as environmental cues, and progress feedback as spatial audio.

Simulation

Inside the Flood

Rising water levels, environmental debris, and spatial audio cues combine to create a high-fidelity stress response. The simulation was validated with test groups who reported feeling genuine urgency — confirming the presence threshold needed for effective safety training.
